What we actually measure

Most AI voice tools read your writing style. We map your linguistic fingerprint — the same framework used in forensic authorship attribution and computational personality science.

01

Idiolect Extraction

Every speaker leaves a fingerprint: the vocabulary they reach for under pressure, the sentence structures they default to, the discourse markers that stitch their thinking together, the words they conspicuously avoid. Two decades of forensic and computational linguistics research has shown these markers identify individual communicators with 60–91% accuracy. We catalog each pattern and flag whether it holds across contexts (trait-level) or only surfaces in specific formats (state-level) — the difference between a usable profile and an anecdotal one.

02

Rhetorical Architecture

Beyond vocabulary, we map how you build a case. Do you open with data, analogy, or inversion? What counts as proof in your hierarchy of evidence? How do you handle disagreement? These structural patterns are among the most cognitively stable features of a communicator — persisting across topics, audiences, and formats. We extract them as repeatable structural sequences, not vague descriptors like "conversational" or "direct."

03

Psychographic Scoring

We apply the Big Five OCEAN framework — the most empirically validated model in personality psychology, cross-culturally replicated across decades of research — scored directly from observable speech behavior, not surveys or self-reports. Pitch variability, speaking rate, and prosodic patterns predict perceived personality traits at 70%+ accuracy. We layer in four dimensions OCEAN misses: how you establish what's true, how you claim authority, how you calibrate to your audience, and when you push against consensus.

When multiple recordings are available, we perform a cross-corpus synthesis — the same methodological principle used in forensic authorship attribution — separating stable cognitive patterns from format-specific adaptations. The result is a profile built from what your language reveals, not what you'd say about yourself.
